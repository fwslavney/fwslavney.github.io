Online Lectures:

Lecture 1 - Introduction to Organic & Why Carbon

Lecture 3 - Structural Formulas; Resonance; Arrow Pushing

Lecture 4 - Resonance Wrap-up; Mechanism/Arrow-pushing

Lecture 5 - Acid-Base Chemistry; Anion Stability Patterns

Lecture 6 - VSEPR 3D Shape; Drawing 3D; Hybridization; Pi bonds; Isomers

Lecture 7 - Polarity IMF; Boiling Points; Solubility; Functional Groups

Lecture 8 - Functional Groups & Alkane Nomenclature

Lecture 9 - Newman Projections; Cycloalkanes

Lecture 10 - Cyclohexane chairs, Cis-and-Trans, Structural Isomers

Lecture 11 - Radical Halogenation Mech; Bond Strengths; Radical Stabilities

Lecture 12 - Rate Laws, Transition States, Stability-Reactivity Principles; Radical Brominations. Major product, mechanism, structural isomers.

Lecture 13 - Radical Brominations. Product, mechanism. Stability patterns for radicals/cations/anions. Stability-reactivity principles.; Chiral vs achiral, Enantiomers

Lecture 14 - Chiral Carbons; Chiral vs achiral; Same vs Enantiomers; Attachment Priorities; R/S Designation

Lecture 15 - Chiral Carbons; Attachment Priorities; R/S Designation; Drawing Chiral Molecules.

Lecture 16 - Racemic mixtures and Optical Activity. Conformations + Chirality; Planes of Symmetry; Meso; Cis/Trans Diastereomers vs Enantiomers; Recognizing Chiral vs Meso

Lecture 17 - Stereo (1-19min); Alkyl Halides. Reaction preview; Alkyl/Vinyl/Aryl/Allyl Classification; 1º/2º/3º Classification; overview of SN2/SN1/E2/E1 reactions; SN2 reactions and mechanism

Lecture 18 - SN2 Reaction. Product Prediction, Structural Factors. Ranking. Inversion. Mechanisms.

Lecture 19 - SN1 reactions

Lecture 20 - E1, E2 Reactions. Zaytsev's Rule. Using Anion/Neutral and 1º, 2º, 3º to diagnose among SN2/E2/SN1/E1

Lecture 21 - E2 Stereochemistry; Diagnosing Reaction Types; Two-Step Synthesis Design; E1 + E2 Mechanisms; Ranking Problems

Lecture 22 - Nonreactive "Strong acid" anions.; p1-5. Intro to alkenes, (EU), Hydrogenation; Alkene Nomenclature; E/Z stereochem

Lecture 23 - Alkene Stabilities; Heats of Hydrogenation/Combustion; Bulky Bases/Normal Bases for Hofmann/Zaytsev Elimination

Lecture 24 - Alkene Formation. From Alcohols via Acid-Catalyzed E1. Mechanism Recognition.

Lecture 25 - Overview, Intro, Addition reactions to Alkenes. Addition of (Markovnickov and Anti-Markovnikov); Acid-Catalyzed HOH Addn;
